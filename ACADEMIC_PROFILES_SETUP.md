## 2. ResearchGate

ResearchGate is a social networking site for scientists and researchers to share papers, ask questions, and find collaborators.

### Steps to Set Up:
1.  **Go to:** [researchgate.net](https://www.researchgate.net/)
2.  **Click:** "Join for free"
3.  **Select Category:** "Not a researcher" (if you don't have an institutional email yet) or "Academic/Student" if you have a .edu email.
    *   *Note:* ResearchGate can be strict about requiring an institutional email. If you struggle to join as an independent researcher, you can try registering as a "Corporate/Government/NGO" researcher if applicable to your Navy background, or look for the "Independent Researcher" flow if they currently offer it.
4.  **Complete Profile:**
    *   Add your skills (Optics, Python, Arduino, etc.)
5.  **Add Research:**
    *   Click "Add new" (top right)
    *   Select "Preprint" (since your paper is on Zenodo)
    *   Upload your PDF (`Wavelength_Ternary_Computing-4.pdf`)
    *   **DOI:** Use your paper DOI `10.5281/zenodo.18437600`
    *   **License:** CC BY 4.0 (Allows you to share it freely)
6.  **Add Software:**
    *   You can also add your software as "Code" or "Data" using the software DOI.

## 4. OSF (Open Science Framework)

OSF is great for managing the entire research lifecycle and integrates with GitHub.

### Steps to Set Up:
1.  **Go to:** [osf.io](https://osf.io/)
2.  **Sign Up:** Free account.
3.  **Create a New Project:** "Wavelength-Division Ternary Optical Computer"
4.  **Add-ons:**
    *   Go to Project Settings â†’ Add-ons
    *   Enable **GitHub**
    *   Connect to your `jackwayne234/-wavelength-ternary-optical-computer` repo.
5.  **Description:** Copy from your README.
6.  **Make Public:** Toggle the project to Public.
7.  **DOI:** OSF can also generate a DOI for the project itself if you want a top-level identifier for the whole initiative (optional since you have Zenodo).
